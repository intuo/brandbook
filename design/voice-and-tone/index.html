Voice and tone

It’s important to think about our users. Though our voice doesn’t change much, our tone adapts to our users’ feelings. It also communicates what our products and our brand are all about.

Voice

Intuo's voice is human. It’s familiar, friendly, and straightforward. Our priority is explaining our products and helping our users get their work done so they can get on with their lives. We want to educate people without patronizing or confusing them.

Tips

  • Fun but not silly
  • Confident but not cocky
  • Smart but not stodgy
  • Informal but not sloppy
  • Helpful but not overbearing
  • Expert but not bossy
  • Weird but not inappropriate

Twitter, Facebook

Talent management doesn't have to be a pain. Here are a few ways Intuo can help: eepurl.com/bdNcjv

Tips

  • Get to the point.
  • Feel free to be casual.
  • Delight our followers with good news when you can.
  • During major news events, turn off scheduled and promoted tweets.

Success message

Fine piece of work! You deserve a raise.

Tips

  • Pat these users on the back for getting a course out the door.
  • Feel free to be casual.
  • They’re probably feeling happy and relieved—use casual language that encourages those feelings.
  • Feel free to be funny.

Be encouraging

What the user thinks

My students seem to like my course. Sweet!

How we should react

Your student enrollment rate was 76% higher than your industry average. Nice work!

Tips

  • Encourage and support users as they complete the process.
  • Tracking is usually the most exciting part for users. Our personality should come through in this step.

Blog

What the user thinks

I learn something every time I read Intuo's blog.

How we should react

We’ve added the blended learning to courses, so you can now take Intuo anywhere. The feature will be available, by the end of next week. We’re pretty excited about this.

Tips

  • These people know Intuo. Be casual with them, and have a sense of humor.
  • Every post should teach readers something. Enlighten them with extra links, tips, information that makes them smarter, or sneak peeks into our office.
  • Assume that readers are smart and tech-savvy Intuo users.

Public site

What the user thinks

Talent management is just what I need. I might sign up for an Intuo account. I wonder what features they have, and how much it’ll cost me per month.

How we should react

Join more than 120 companies who use Intuo to track and increase the human capital of their company every day.

Tips

  • Be confident in explaining why Intuo is so great.
  • Show people how easy it is to use Intuo.
  • Delight people with design, data, and helpful information about Intuo.
  • Relay our personality and sense of humor, but don’t go crazy (think clever, not silly).

Legal content

What the user thinks

I need to make sure Intuo isn’t going to steal my content before I publish my courses.

How we should react

You retain ownership of the materials you upload to the Service. We may use or disclose your materials only as we describe in these Terms and our Privacy Policy.

Tips

  • Write as calmly and clearly as possible, within the obvious constraints. Only use legal jargon when it’s absolutely necessary.
  • Don’t dumb anything down or joke around. Legal content is serious business.

Calming the user

What the user thinks

I need to get this course published ASAP, because my boss is breathing down my back.

How we should react

Send now—this is your moment of glory.

Tips

  • Use language that empowers people to create courses.
  • Encourage and support users as they go through a potentially stressful process.
  • Provide a little comic relief, but don’t annoy or distract busy users with over-the-top humor or too many extra words.

Failure message

What the user thinks

What went wrong? I really need to get this course published.

How we should react

We’re experiencing a brief problem. Our engineers are on the case, and will have things back to normal shortly.

Tips

  • Offer a solution or next step.
  • Be straightforward. Explain what’s going on right away.
  • Be calm. Don’t use exclamation points or alarming words like "alert" or "immediately."
  • Be serious. Don’t joke around with people who are frustrated.
